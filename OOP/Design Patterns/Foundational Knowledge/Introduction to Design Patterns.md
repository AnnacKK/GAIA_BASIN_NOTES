[[Design Patterns]]

A **design pattern** is a practical, proven solution to a recurring software design problem encountered by industry experts over time[cite: 1]. Think of them like recipes in a cookbook: they are heavily tested, standardized strategies rather than copy-paste libraries or frameworks[cite: 1].

## Key Advantages
* **Avoids Over-engineering**: You do not have to build solutions from basic object-oriented principles every single time[cite: 1].
* **Design Vocabulary**: Provides a simplified, naming-standardized means of discussing design solutions among developers without misinterpretations[cite: 1].
* **Untangles Spaghetti Code**: Aids in restructuring highly disorganized, structureless source code[cite: 1].


---

## 📚 The Gang of Four (GoF) Catalogue
 formalized by Erich Gamma, Richard Helm, Ralph Johnson, and John Vlissides, GoF patterns function similarly to literary tropes (like the "hero's journey")—recurring architectural structures found across thousands of distinct systems[cite: 1].

### Pattern Languages
A **pattern language** is a specific collection of interconnected patterns tied to a explicit problem domain[cite: 1]. For instance, accounting software pattern languages rely on double-entry bookkeeping terms, whereas gaming software pattern languages rely on players, quests, and encounters[cite: 1].

## 🗂️ The Three Primary Categories

| Category | Primary Focus | Metaphor |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Creational** | Handles how objects are created or cloned, heavily dependent on compiler class rules versus runtime prototypes[cite: 1]. | Blueprints & Manufacturing |
| **Structural** | Describes how objects connect, interact, and establish relationships via inheritance, decomposition, and generalization[cite: 1]. | Food Pairings (e.g., wine and cheese or mixed ingredients)[cite: 1]. |
| **Behavioural** | Focuses on how independent objects distribute work and execute cohesive functions toward a common goal[cite: 1]. | A Racing Car Pit Crew working in unison[cite: 1]. |
